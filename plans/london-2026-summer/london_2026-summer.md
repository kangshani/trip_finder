## Essential Tips

### Transport
- **Get Oyster cards** or use **contactless payment** for Tube/bus. Kids under 11 travel FREE on Tube/bus with a paying adult
- **Buy a Family & Friends Railcard** (£30) - saves 1/3 on train fares and 60% off kids' fares for day trips
- **Thames Clippers** accept Oyster/contactless - a fun alternative to the Tube

### Money-Saving Tips
- **Book attraction tickets online** in advance - usually 10-20% cheaper
- **London Pass** - may be worth it if doing many paid attractions. Calculate before buying
- **Free museums**: Natural History, Science, British Museum, V&A, National Gallery, Tate Modern - all free!
- **Picnic lunches** in parks save £20-30/day vs restaurants
- **Supermarkets** (Tesco, Sainsbury's, M&S Food) do great meal deals (~£5/person)

### June-Specific Tips
- **Daylight**: Sunrise ~4:45, sunset ~21:20 - incredibly long days, maximize outdoor time
- **Weather**: Average 15-22°C, can reach 28°C+. Pack layers + light rain jacket
- **Trooping the Colour** (mid-June): King's birthday parade - spectacular but very crowded around the Mall
- **Wimbledon** starts late June: Queue for ground passes if interested
- **Parks & gardens** are at their best - roses blooming, long evenings perfect for picnics

### Kid-Specific Tips
- **Carry snacks & water** always - London is a walking city
- **Rest breaks**: Schedule downtime after lunch (Day 3 & 7 have built-in rest)
- **Tube tip**: Avoid rush hours (8-9:30, 17-18:30) with kids
- **Rainy day backup**: Science Museum, Natural History Museum, or Hamleys toy store
- **Buggy/stroller**: London Tube has many stairs - check TfL step-free access guide

### Booking in Advance (Recommended)
- London Eye (1-2 weeks ahead)
- Tower of London (1 week ahead)
- Windsor Castle (book online morning of)
- SEA LIFE Aquarium (online for discount)
- Restaurant reservations for Dishoom (2+ weeks - it's very popular!)
